## 1. Thesis

*A serial ADC team that already built and sold a company to Eli Lilly is doing it again, in the hottest modality in oncology, with a validated payload for the lead asset and novel payloads behind it — this is a repeat-outcome bet where the exit path is proven.*

The rarest thing in biotech is a team that has *already done the exact thing you're underwriting them to do*. Adcytherix is the Emergence Therapeutics team — acquired by Eli Lilly in 2023 — running the same play in antibody-drug conjugates, a modality where big pharma is spending tens of billions to build pipelines. The bet is less "does the science work" and more "does this proven team execute a proven playbook in a market actively shopping for exactly this."

## 2. What they do

Antibody-drug conjugates (ADCs) are antibodies that home to a tumour, linked to a potent toxic "payload" that is delivered directly into cancer cells while sparing healthy tissue. Adcytherix's strategy is to compete **not on the antibody but on the payload** — building ADCs for patients who have become resistant to today's ADC classes.

- **Lead: ADCX-020** — a stable-linker ADC using a clinically validated exatecan-derived topoisomerase-I inhibitor payload, designed for a wide therapeutic index across solid tumours.
- **Pipeline:** additional ADCs against validated antigens using **novel-mechanism payloads** — the intended long-term differentiation.

**Stage:** **clinical-stage as of March 2026** — first patient dosed in the Phase 1a/b trial of ADCX-020, less than two years after inception. This materially de-risks the company versus a pure pre-clinical bet.

## 3. Market

- The **ADC market** is one of oncology's fastest-growing, in the tens of billions of dollars and rising, with relentless M&A and licensing (e.g. Daiichi Sankyo/AstraZeneca's Enhertu franchise, Pfizer/Seagen, AbbVie/ImmunoGen).
- **Target:** high-unmet-need solid tumours, explicitly including patients **resistant to existing ADCs** — a growing population as first-generation ADCs become standard of care.
- **Who pays:** big pharma, both as commercial partner and as acquirer. The team's own history (Emergence → Lilly) *is* the exit template.

**Price discipline note.** This is a large, crossover-heavy round; our achievable ownership (~0.8%) is well below target, and the entry price already reflects the team's pedigree. I am comfortable here for two reasons: (1) the clinical-stage status and validated lead payload lower the risk that we are paying for hope alone, and (2) the exit path is unusually concrete. But this is the position where I most need to be honest that **we are a price-taker on a hot deal** — reserves (1.5x) exist to build ownership at Series B once Phase 1 data starts to read. If allocation isn't available on clean terms, pass.

## 6. Risks (pre-mortem)

1. **Phase 1 disappoints (primary risk).** ADCX-020 only entered humans in March 2026. ADCs carry real toxicity risk; safety, therapeutic index, or early efficacy could underwhelm. Everything rests on clinical data now.
2. **Differentiation is still on paper.** The lead uses a *crowded* validated payload class (exatecan/topo-I); the "novel payload" edge that justifies the premium is still pre-clinical.
3. **Brutal competition.** Daiichi/AstraZeneca, Pfizer/Seagen, AbbVie, plus a wave of cheaper China-originated ADCs. Differentiation must be shown *clinically*, not claimed.
4. **Expectations priced in.** Investors are underwriting a repeat of the Lilly outcome; that raises the bar and the price, and repeats are not guaranteed.
5. **Capital intensity.** Oncology clinical development is expensive; further large raises or a partnership will be needed to reach registration.

## 7. The bet & the plan

**Conviction: highest of the life-science sleeve.** The combination of a proven, exit-generating team, a validated modality and lead payload, clinical-stage status, and a concrete acquirer pool is as de-risked as early-stage oncology gets. The trade-off is a high entry price and thin ownership.

**What I do in the 18 months after wiring:**
- **Track the Phase 1a/b dose-escalation closely** — safety and therapeutic-index signals are the leading indicator of both value and of whether to deploy reserves.
- **Deploy reserves at Series B / crossover** to lift ownership toward a meaningful stake *if* early clinical data holds.
- **Watch the M&A tape** for read-throughs on ADC valuations and acquirer appetite; the exit window for this asset may open on someone else's deal.
- **Pressure-test the novel-payload pipeline** — that, not ADCX-020, is what makes Adcytherix a platform rather than a single-asset story.

**Kill criteria:** dose-limiting toxicity or absent efficacy signal in Phase 1a with no viable path in the follow-on payloads → no follow-on, mark down.
