## 1. Thesis

*Most of how a company actually works is never written down; if Interloom's "Context Graph" can capture that tacit knowledge and give AI agents a durable memory of it, it becomes the system of record for enterprise work — a far larger prize than replacing rule-based RPA.*

Robotic process automation (RPA) failed to deliver on its promise because rigid if-then rules can't handle the messy, undocumented reality of enterprise work. Interloom's bet is that the missing layer is **memory** — a living map of how problems actually get solved, learned from operational data and expert humans. If that's right, Interloom sits underneath the entire agentic-AI wave as critical infrastructure. This is a high-variance infrastructure bet, which is why the position is deliberately small.

## 2. What they do

Interloom ingests millions of operational records — support emails, tickets, call transcripts, work orders — and infers **how problems actually get solved**, building a proprietary **"Context Graph"**: a live map of cases, decisions, experts, and outcomes (the company's analogy is "Google Maps for work"). Human experts resolve complex escalations, and those resolutions are captured as durable "memory." The pitch is explicitly beyond rigid RPA: agents that learn the organisation rather than follow brittle scripts.

- **Target customer:** large enterprises with high-volume, complex operations — insurance, banking, logistics, customer service. Started with the German Mittelstand.
- **Stage:** in market with named deployments but still pre-full-launch (beta); full product flagged for later 2026.

## 3. Market

- **RPA** is a $6B+/year market growing double digits, dominated by UiPath — but widely criticised for underdelivering, which is precisely the opening.
- Tailwinds (some company-sourced): a large majority of operational decisions are never documented; the "great retirement" is draining institutional knowledge from enterprises; agentic AI needs exactly this context layer to be useful.
- **Who pays:** large enterprises seeking to automate complex work that rules-based RPA couldn't touch — and to preserve knowledge that is walking out the door.

## 4. Team

- **Fabian Jakobi (CEO, founder)** — serial entrepreneur; previously founded Boxplot (sold to Hyperscience, 2021). A prior exit in adjacent enterprise-automation territory is the core reason to back him.
- Founding-team composition is **ambiguous across sources** (CTO Miha Erzen and Head of Sales Jaime Madrid are listed as co-founders in some databases) — a diligence flag.
- Backed by **Air Street Capital** (seed), then **DN Capital** (lead) and **Bek Ventures** (an early UiPath backer — relevant domain signal) in the extension.

There is **no disclosed revenue/ARR**, so the estimated ~$65M post is underwritten on team and narrative, not traction. That justifies a **small** initial check and a high bar for follow-on. This is the most speculative of the opportunistic three; I size it accordingly.

## 6. Risks (pre-mortem)

1. **Hyperscaler encroachment (primary risk).** OpenAI, Microsoft, and ServiceNow are all building agent-memory/context layers. If "context for agents" becomes a feature of the model platforms, Interloom's standalone moat erodes. The Context Graph advantage is asserted, not yet proven durable.
2. **No ground truth / hard to audit.** Unlike software with a compiler, the quality of learned "how work gets done" depends on human experts and is difficult to verify and scale — and risky in regulated settings.
3. **Compliance friction.** Ingesting banks' and insurers' emails, tickets, and call transcripts raises serious GDPR/data-residency questions not publicly addressed.
4. **Adoption inertia & long cycles.** The CEO himself names enterprise inertia as the #1 competitor; full launch is still pending, and enterprise sales are slow.
5. **Thin financial visibility.** No disclosed revenue; a slow ramp could burn the raise before the model is proven.

## 7. The bet & the plan

**Conviction: watchful, deliberately small.** The prize — becoming the memory/context layer beneath enterprise AI — is genuinely large, and a founder with a relevant exit is the right person to chase it. But the moat is unproven and the hyperscaler threat is real, so this is a *small option* on a big outcome, not a core position.

**What I do in the 18 months after wiring:**
- **Diligence the founding team and cap table** to resolve the co-founder ambiguity before any follow-on.
- **Demand evidence the Context Graph is a moat:** does accuracy compound with data in a way competitors can't quickly replicate? Track retention and expansion in the named accounts (Zurich, Commerzbank, VW).
- **Watch the hyperscalers** — if the model platforms ship a credible agent-memory layer, reassess immediately.
- **Keep the position small** and treat follow-on as conditional on a genuine full-launch with paying, expanding customers and a clear compliance story.

**Kill criteria:** a hyperscaler ships a good-enough context/memory layer, *or* full launch reveals weak paid retention → no follow-on, likely write-down.
