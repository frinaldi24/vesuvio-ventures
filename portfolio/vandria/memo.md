## 1. Thesis

*If restoring the body's ability to clear damaged mitochondria (mitophagy) can modify age-related disease, Vandria has a first-in-class, oral, brain-penetrant drug in a validated-mechanism, multi-indication platform — and it already has positive Phase 1 human data de-risking the mechanism.*

Most Alzheimer's bets are binary science bets with no human data. Vandria is different: it is **clinical-stage**, and in December 2025 it reported **positive Phase 1 target-engagement data** — the drug is safe and demonstrably hits its target in humans. That doesn't prove efficacy, but it removes a whole layer of "does the molecule even do anything in people" risk that sinks most early neuro programs. The mechanism (mitophagy) is also not a one-disease story — it plausibly extends to muscle, lung, and liver disease.

## 2. What they do

Vandria makes **oral small molecules that induce mitophagy** — the cell's process for selectively removing and replacing damaged mitochondria — while also dampening inflammation. Damaged mitochondria accumulate with age and drive many age-related diseases; restoring their turnover is the therapeutic idea.

- **Lead: VNA-318** — a first-in-class, orally bioavailable, **brain-penetrant** mitophagy inducer with a dual mode of action: potential **acute cognitive improvement** plus **long-term disease modification** (less neuroinflammation, less toxic protein aggregation). Lead indication: **Alzheimer's**.
- **Platform:** additional mitophagy inducers for muscle, lung, and liver disease, plus a separate ferroptosis program.

**Stage:** **clinical.** First-in-human Phase 1 dosed December 2024; **positive Phase 1 topline December 2025** (well tolerated, no serious adverse events, statistically significant dose-dependent target-engagement biomarker). Phase 1b/2a proof-of-concept studies are next.

## 3. Market

- **Alzheimer's** market ~$7.6B and growing (~12% CAGR to 2035, per the company), driven by ageing populations and better diagnosis.
- Broader **geroscience** thesis: mitochondrial dysfunction is a shared driver across age-related disease, giving the platform optionality well beyond Alzheimer's — a framing that attracted healthspan-focused backers (Hevolution).
- **Who pays:** pharma, via partnership or acquisition once proof-of-concept efficacy is shown. A differentiated *oral* neuro asset with disease-modifying potential is a scarce and valuable object.

## 6. Risks (pre-mortem)

1. **Target engagement ≠ efficacy (primary risk).** Phase 1 shows the drug is safe and hits its target; it does **not** show it helps patients. Alzheimer's has one of the highest clinical failure rates in medicine, and many mechanistically sound drugs die at Phase 2.
2. **Mechanism unvalidated clinically.** Mitophagy induction as a *disease-modifying* strategy is unproven in humans; Vandria is effectively pioneering the clinical validation of the whole mechanism.
3. **Financing risk.** ~$30.7M raised; the Phase 2 program depends on a Series B that has not closed. A stalled raise could halt momentum right after the good Phase 1 news.
4. **Crowded, high-bar field.** Approved anti-amyloid antibodies set an efficacy benchmark; a new mechanism must clear it convincingly.

## 7. The bet & the plan

**Conviction: high, and the best risk-adjusted entry in the sleeve.** Vandria pairs a genuinely novel, multi-indication mechanism with something most early neuro bets lack — *positive human data*. The Phase 1 readout meaningfully narrows the range of outcomes and gives a concrete catalyst schedule to underwrite against.

**What I do in the 18 months after wiring:**
- **Underwrite to the Phase 1b/2a proof-of-concept** — this is the value-inflection event; define with the company what an investable efficacy signal looks like.
- **Manage financing risk actively:** help syndicate and de-risk the Series B; do not let a strong asset stall for lack of capital. Reserve deployment is conditional on a credible B.
- **Value the platform, not just the lead:** the muscle/lung/liver optionality is where a single Alzheimer's outcome becomes a multi-shot platform — track whether the company advances a second indication.
- **Line up pharma interest** ahead of proof-of-concept, since an oral disease-modifying neuro asset is exactly what large pharma shops for.

**Kill criteria:** Phase 1b/2a shows no efficacy signal *and* the platform fails to advance a credible second indication → no Series B follow-on, mark down.
