# Vesuvio Ventures

**€50M early-stage venture fund · Vintage 2026 · Europe**
*Life sciences at the core, opportunistic elsewhere.*

> **What this is.** Vesuvio Ventures is a **simulated** venture capital fund I run as a personal project to think, decide, and write like an early-stage investor. Every company here is real, but **I have not invested in any of them** — there is no capital, no legal entity, and no relationship with the founders or their backers. All analysis is built exclusively from **publicly available information** and reflects my own judgment. See the [full disclaimer](#disclaimer).

I built Vesuvio because the best way to learn the craft of investing is to actually do the work: source a deal, size a market, argue a thesis, price a round, name the risks that would kill it, and then hold yourself accountable as reality plays out. This repository is that work, in the open.

## How I decide

Every memo follows the same spine, because a consistent process is what separates judgment from enthusiasm:

1. **Thesis** — the one sentence that has to be true for this to be a fund-returner.
2. **What they do** — in plain language, no jargon smuggling.
3. **Market** — how big, how contested, and who ultimately pays.
4. **Team** — why *these* founders, and what they've already proven.
5. **Deal** — the round, my simulated entry, ownership, and price discipline.
6. **Risks** — the specific ways I lose all my money, ranked honestly.
7. **The bet & the plan** — recommendation, conviction level, and what I'd do in the 18 months after wiring.
