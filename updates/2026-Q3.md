## Position-by-position

### Aerska
- **Mark:** Cost. Pre-clinical; no readout yet to re-rate.
- **News this quarter:** Closed the $39M Series A (Feb 2026); building toward a first delivery readout.
- **Kill criteria status:** Clear (nothing to test yet).
- **Reserve decision:** Hold 1.5x reserves for a Series B *conditional on* the pre-clinical brain-delivery readout.
- **Was I right?** Too early. The whole bet is the delivery data that hasn't come.

### Adcytherix
- **Mark:** Slight markup (qualitative). First patient dosed in ADCX-020 Phase 1 (Mar 2026) — clinical-stage status is a real de-risking step versus the seed.
- **News this quarter:** Phase 1a/b underway; leadership strengthened (COO, CRQO, independent chairman).
- **Kill criteria status:** Clear; watching dose-escalation safety.
- **Reserve decision:** Hold 1.5x for Series B/crossover; deploy on clean early clinical data.
- **Was I right?** So far yes — the team executed into the clinic fast, as underwritten. Value now hinges on data.

### Seamless Therapeutics
- **Mark:** Markup (qualitative). The Lilly collaboration (up to $1.12B, Jan 2026) is exactly the platform validation the thesis required.
- **News this quarter:** Lilly deal in motion; platform still pre-clinical.
- **Kill criteria status:** Clear.
- **Reserve decision:** Deploy 2x reserves into the Series A — validation has lowered platform risk; this is the position I'd lean into.
- **Was I right?** The asymmetric-platform call is playing out; a second pharma deal would confirm it.

### Vandria
- **Mark:** Markup (qualitative). Positive Phase 1 target-engagement data (Dec 2025) removed a layer of "does it do anything in humans" risk.
- **News this quarter:** Advancing toward Phase 1b/2a; Series B planned.
- **Kill criteria status:** Clear; financing risk on the unclosed Series B is the live concern.
- **Reserve decision:** Hold 1.5x for the Series B, *conditional on a credible raise*; support syndication.
- **Was I right?** Best risk-adjusted entry so far — human data in hand. Proof-of-concept efficacy is the next test.

### Pit
- **Mark:** Cost. Just out of stealth (May 2026); no arm's-length traction data yet.
- **News this quarter:** $16M a16z-led seed announced; early customers largely founder-network.
- **Kill criteria status:** Clear; watching for services-vs-software margin shape.
- **Reserve decision:** Hold 1x for Series A, gated on non-network net revenue retention.
- **Was I right?** Unproven. Strong signal, no business evidence yet — as expected at entry.

### Interloom
- **Mark:** Cost. Pre-full-launch; no disclosed revenue.
- **News this quarter:** $16.5M seed extension (Mar 2026); still beta.
- **Kill criteria status:** Watch — hyperscaler agent-memory encroachment is the standing threat.
- **Reserve decision:** Hold small; follow-on strictly gated on moat evidence + paid full launch.
- **Was I right?** Deliberately the smallest position; conviction remains watchful, as underwritten.

### Onodrim Industries
- **Mark:** Cost. Pre-product; richly priced entry.
- **News this quarter:** €40M seed (Feb 2026); no product, revenue, or contracts yet.
- **Kill criteria status:** Watch — the clock on a first real contract has started.
- **Reserve decision:** Hold 1x, gated on a first contract/funded pilot; realistic that later capital intensity may exceed our ability to defend ownership.
- **Was I right?** Thesis intact, execution entirely ahead. This is the position I'm watching hardest.

## Portfolio-level reflections

- **Shape of the book is working as intended.** Risk is spread across binary science (Aerska, Seamless), de-risked clinical (Adcytherix, Vandria), and execution/market bets (Pit, Interloom, Onodrim). No single failure mode takes down the portfolio.
- **The de-risked names are pulling ahead early.** Adcytherix, Seamless, and Vandria each hit a real validating event (clinical entry, pharma deal, human data). If I had to concentrate reserves today, they'd lead — Seamless first.
- **Ownership discipline cost me size on the hot deals** (Aerska, Adcytherix, Onodrim all below target ownership). That's the deliberate trade-off of not overpaying; reserves are the mechanism to rebuild ownership on evidence.
- **Anti-portfolio:** AMI recorded as a pass on fund-fit and price. No regret yet, but flagged to revisit.
- **Construction:** allocation is on-target (~60/40); pacing is early in the 2026–2028 investment window. No adjustments needed.
