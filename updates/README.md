# Quarterly Updates

*Vesuvio Ventures · How the portfolio tracks over time.*

An investor's memos are only worth as much as their willingness to mark them to reality. Each quarter I record how every position is moving, what news changed the thesis, whether I'd deploy reserves, and — most importantly — **where my original memo was wrong**.

Marks use a simple, honest convention:

- **Cost** — held at entry value; no new evidence to re-rate.
- **Markup / Markdown** — thesis progressing or deteriorating; reflected in a directional mark.
- **Write-off** — a kill criterion from the memo has been triggered.

These are *simulated, judgment-based* marks for a learning exercise, not audited valuations.

## How to read a quarterly update

Each entry covers, per company: the mark and its direction, the news since last quarter, whether any kill criterion is close, the reserve decision, and a one-line "was I right?" reflection on the entry thesis.
