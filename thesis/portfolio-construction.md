# Portfolio Construction

*Vesuvio Ventures · €50M · Vintage 2026*

How €50M is meant to be deployed. Portfolio construction is where a thesis becomes a discipline: it forces every individual "yes" to fit inside a plan for the whole fund.

The split is a **target, not a rule**. If a vintage produces exceptional deals weighted one way, we follow the quality — but we treat any drift beyond ~10 points as a decision to justify, not an accident to explain away.

## Deployment math

A €50M fund cannot behave like a €500M fund. The construction is built backwards from ownership and reserves:

- **~55% deployed at entry** (~€27.5M) across **~15–20 companies**.
- **~45% reserved** (~€22.5M) for follow-on into the companies that are working.
- **Initial checks: €1.0M–€3.0M**, sized to reach **5–12% ownership** at entry.
- **Reserves ratio ~1:0.8** of initial-to-follow-on on the positions we choose to double down on.

The reserve strategy is the heart of the model. Early-stage returns are driven by a small number of positions; the job is to *recognise* them early enough to concentrate more capital into them, and to have the dry powder to do so. Reserves are allocated at the portfolio's mid-life based on evidence, not spread evenly at entry.

## Ownership and why it matters

At seed and Series A we target **5–12% ownership**. Below ~5%, even a fund-returning outcome barely moves our numbers; above ~12% in a first cheque, we are usually overpaying or over-concentrating for the stage. Ownership is defended, not just bought: our reserves exist partly to maintain position through later dilutive rounds in our winners.

## Return model

The fund is underwritten to a **power-law outcome**, not an average one:

- We assume **most positions return less than 1x**. That is not failure of the model; it is the model.
- The fund is designed so that **one or two fund-returners** — a position that returns ≥1x the entire €50M on its own — drive the result.
- Target gross fund return: **3–5x** over a 7–10 year horizon, implying at least one outcome in the **€150M+ proceeds** range from a single position.

Every entry memo therefore ends with the same test: *plausibly, can this one return the fund?* A company can be excellent and still be a "no" if the honest answer is that it cannot.

## Pacing

Capital is deployed over roughly a **3-year investment period** (2026–2028), with follow-on reserves deployed across years 2–6. Pacing protects against two classic errors: deploying the whole fund into a single frothy window, and running out of reserves before the winners reveal themselves.

## The seven flagship positions

The companies profiled in depth here represent the fund's **conviction core** — the positions I would defend in an investment committee. They are deliberately spread across:

- **Stage:** from pre-clinical platform (Seamless) to clinical-stage with human data (Adcytherix, Vandria).
- **Risk shape:** binary scientific bets (life sciences) alongside execution-and-market bets (opportunistic).
- **Modality and sector:** RNAi, ADCs, gene editing, small molecules; enterprise AI, automation, defence-industrial.

This spread is intentional. A concentrated fund still needs its risks to be *uncorrelated* — a single clinical failure, funding-market freeze, or AI-hype correction should not take down the whole book.

## Governance of the process

- **One consistent memo structure** for every deal, so decisions are comparable.
- **A written pre-mortem** in every memo: the specific way this loses all its money.
- **An anti-portfolio** kept as seriously as the portfolio, because what you decline defines a fund as much as what you back.
- **Quarterly marks** with honest write-ups, including where the original thesis was wrong.
