# Investment Thesis

*Vesuvio Ventures · €50M · Vintage 2026*

---

## In one paragraph

Europe produces world-class science and, increasingly, world-class engineers — but it has historically under-performed at turning that raw material into companies that scale. That gap is the opportunity. Vesuvio Ventures backs **early-stage European founders** at the point where a genuine technical or scientific edge meets a credible commercial path, with **60% of capital committed to life sciences** — where deep sector understanding creates real edge in selection — and **40% held opportunistically** for exceptional founders in software, AI, and industrial technology. We invest at seed and Series A, take meaningful minority positions, and reserve heavily to back our winners.

---

## Why life sciences, and why now

Three structural shifts make European early-stage life science an attractive place to deploy patient capital in this vintage:

**The tools have compounded.** RNA delivery, gene editing, ADCs, and machine-learning-assisted discovery have each crossed from "promising" to "clinically validated" in the last five years. Founders can now credibly target diseases that were untouchable a decade ago. Validation reduces one category of risk (does the modality work at all?) while leaving the category where investors are actually paid: execution and selection.

**Capital is concentrated, which favours the disciplined.** European biotech funding is lumpy and cyclical; in a typical month a handful of rounds account for most of the money. When capital is scarce and clustered around a few names, a fund that can independently judge quality — rather than follow momentum — can enter good companies at rational prices.

**Pharma is buying, not building.** Large pharma increasingly outsources early innovation and acquires it later, evidenced by a steady flow of multi-hundred-million and billion-euro-plus licensing deals and acquisitions of pre-clinical and clinical-stage European biotechs. That creates a real, repeatable exit path for well-chosen assets, often before a product reaches the market.

Our edge in this sleeve is **selection**, not capital: understanding the difference between a validated modality and a validated *company*, reading whether a team can actually reach the clinic, and pricing binary scientific risk without either romanticising the science or dismissing it.

## Why 40% opportunistic

A pure-thematic fund leaves returns on the table. The single best deal a European seed fund sees in a vintage is often *not* in its core theme — it is an exceptional founder in an adjacent category. The opportunistic sleeve exists to say yes to those without diluting the discipline of the core. It is **founder-led, not theme-led**: we require an unusual team, a defensible edge, and a market large enough to return the fund. In practice this sleeve skews toward AI-native software, enterprise automation, and European industrial and defence technology, where a sovereignty tailwind and a deep industrial base give European startups a structural reason to exist.

## What we look for

- **A real edge.** A scientific mechanism, a data asset, a technical architecture, or a founder-market fit that a fast follower cannot trivially copy.
- **A fund-returner outcome is plausible.** We size every deal against the question: *if this works, does it return the fund?* If the honest answer is no, we pass however nice the company is.
- **Founders who compound.** People who have already turned incomplete information and limited resources into progress — the trait matters more than the pedigree.
- **A rational entry price.** We would rather miss a great company than overpay for it. Price is the one risk fully within our control.

## What we avoid

- **Great science, no company.** A beautiful platform with no line of sight to a product, a payer, or a partner.
- **Momentum priced to perfection.** Rounds where the valuation already assumes the risk is gone. (See the [anti-portfolio](../anti-portfolio/README.md).)
- **Capital-out-of-scope bets.** Businesses whose realistic capital needs dwarf what a €50M fund can responsibly support to a meaningful ownership stake.
- **Conflicted deals.** Companies where we could plausibly hold non-public information. Integrity in a small ecosystem is an asset we refuse to spend.

## How we win for founders

We are a **small, high-conviction** fund. That means concentrated attention rather than a spray of tiny cheques: help with the next round's narrative and investor targeting, financial and operational rigour in the messy early years, and honest counsel from someone who has sat on the operating side of an early-stage company. We aim to be the first call when something breaks, not the last.
